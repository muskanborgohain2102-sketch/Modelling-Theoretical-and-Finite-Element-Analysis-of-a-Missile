# Modelling, Theoretical and Finite Element Analysis of a Missile

**Authors:** Sivam Karmakar, Muskan Borgohain, Velpula Tharun  
**Institution:** Tezpur University

---

## Project Overview

This project focused on the multidisciplinary design and computational analysis
of a multistage missile system, integrating propulsion, aerodynamics, structural
analysis, thermal engineering, and stage-separation mechanisms. The objective
was to develop an optimized missile configuration capable of efficient
propulsion, reliable stage separation, thermal resilience, and aerodynamic
stability under demanding operational conditions.

---

## Key Contributions

### Supersonic Nozzle Design

A minimum-length convergent-divergent nozzle was designed using the Method of
Characteristics (MOC) to achieve shock-free and nearly isentropic supersonic
flow. MATLAB-based numerical calculations were employed to generate the nozzle
contour, which was subsequently modelled in SOLIDWORKS and validated using
ANSYS Fluent simulations.

Key outcomes included:
- Exit Mach number ≈ 2.44
- Predicted thrust ≈ 19.6 kN
- Optimized nozzle geometry with reduced divergence losses
- Smooth temperature and Mach number distributions throughout the flow field

### Computational Fluid Dynamics Analysis

Flow simulations were conducted to evaluate aerodynamic and propulsion
performance. Results demonstrated effective acceleration of combustion gases
to supersonic speeds, with Mach number distributions reaching values exceeding
4 in the divergent section. Temperature contours confirmed expected thermal
behaviour within the nozzle during operation.

### Thermal and Structural Analysis

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) was performed on critical components,
particularly the motor mount, to assess thermal stresses, deformation, and
structural integrity under elevated temperature conditions. The study verified
that the design remained within allowable stress limits while maintaining
operational safety.

### Composite Material Integration

Advanced lightweight materials including carbon-fibre composites, graphite-
fibre composites, ceramics, and phenolic materials were evaluated for missile
structures. Material selection emphasized:
- High strength-to-weight ratio
- Thermal resistance
- Fatigue performance
- Structural durability under aerospace loading conditions

### Stage Auxiliary System (SAS) Study

A comprehensive study of Stage Auxiliary Systems was conducted to investigate
reliable stage separation mechanisms and vehicle safety systems. The analysis
considered aerodynamic disturbances, thermal environments, separation dynamics,
and mission-critical reliability requirements for multistage launch vehicles.

## Outcomes

The project successfully demonstrated the integration of theoretical aerospace
engineering principles with computational modelling techniques to design an
efficient and structurally robust missile system. The research contributed to
understanding lightweight composite aerospace structures, supersonic nozzle
optimization, thermal management, and computational design methodologies for
future aerospace applications.
